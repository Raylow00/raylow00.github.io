Why Twelve Floors

Hi! My name is Ray Low, a Mechatronic Engineering student with the passion to tell stories by making games. As far as I am told, I have always been more of a secretive boy growing up, with little to nothing to share with my friends. In fact, when engaged in any conversation with anyone around me, I tend to stay really quiet until prompted to speak, but the words that leave my mouth didn’t necessarily reflect my thoughts. Storytelling has always been a part of my high school life, writing random essays to submit in class, albeit not the most creative one. Today, I am ready to take the creative process to the next level by telling a story through a game.

The old condo

I still remember the old condominium that I used to live in together with my family. It was a little cramped that my brother and I slept on a double-decker bed. We were on the 12th floor, and we enjoyed the breeze every single evening, though the Indian neighbors below us had curry for dinner almost every night. The curry-flavored breeze was unforgettable.

Forget about the dog. I didn't have one.

Curtis, is that you?

Inspired by this old-fashioned but memorable building, Twelve Floors is a 2D adventure game that brings together a story of a kid whose name is Curtis who wakes up one day short of any memories, finds that every piece of truth told is a lie. In fact, every kid who grew up with Curt isn’t behaving like how they used to. Things got weirder when the kids tell him that his dad is dead!

Wrench + Pencil = Wrencil

As Curt, together with a “wrencil” — a unique broken wrench taped to a pencil as your primary tool of defense, you have to discover the truth behind each lie — of course, not all lies are lies, they said. Solve puzzles to unlock new abilities and win boss fights to learn the truth!

Breathing life into characters

Twelve Floors has been a game that I have always wanted to make. In May 2020, this dream turned into a reality when I brought together a team of talented people on board to help breathe life into each character. Two months into puzzle-planning and story development, production has begun and I am truly honored and blessed to have a team like this.
